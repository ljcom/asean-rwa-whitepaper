# Writing Rules – ASEAN Real Estate Tokenization Whitepaper

## 1. Tone & Positioning

- Tone: Regulatory-grade, institutional, credible.
- Audience:
  - Financial regulators (OJK, MAS, SC Malaysia)
  - Institutional investors
  - Policy advisors
  - Exchange operators
- Avoid hype language.
- Avoid DeFi-anarchist framing.
- Emphasize compliance-by-design.

Use:
- enables
- facilitates
- supports
- is structured to comply

Avoid:
- revolutionizes
- disrupts regulators
- eliminates regulation
- guarantees liquidity


---

## 2. Legal Framing

- Token represents economic rights only.
- No land ownership transfer via blockchain.
- Underlying asset jurisdiction remains domestic (Indonesia unless otherwise specified).
- All cross-border logic must respect local securities law.
- Syariah claims must be supported by structural explanation (no riba, asset-backed, risk-sharing).


---

## 3. Compliance Principles

- Hybrid architecture (off-chain KYC, on-chain hash).
- Data minimization by default.
- Personal data never stored on-chain.
- Role-based access control (RBAC).
- Sequential approval for issuance.
- Jurisdiction-aware whitelist.

Always frame architecture as:
> compliance-enabling, not compliance-bypassing.


---

## 4. ASEAN Scope Constraint

- Minimum scope: ASEAN region.
- Cross-border interoperability must consider:
  - Indonesia (OJK, UU PDP)
  - Singapore (MAS, PDPA)
  - Malaysia (SC, PDPA)
- Do not assume global SEC-level expansion.
- Avoid references to permissionless global markets unless clearly contextualized.


---

## 5. Liquidity Framing

- Tokenization ≠ automatic liquidity.
- Liquidity must be engineered.
- Always explain:
  - Exchange integration
  - Market maker model
  - NAV reference
  - Redemption mechanism

Avoid claiming:
- "24/7 liquidity guaranteed"

Instead use:
- "24/7 trading capability subject to exchange adoption"


---

## 6. Funding Model Language

For each model:
- Debt → fixed-income instrument
- Equity → profit participation
- Sukuk → asset-backed, no interest
- Crowdfunding → capped retail participation

Clearly distinguish:
- Risk profile
- Regulatory implication
- Liquidity expectation


---

## 7. Syariah Writing Constraint

If mentioning syariah:
- Avoid using the word "interest"
- Use: profit rate, rental yield, participation return
- Emphasize:
  - asset-backed
  - risk-sharing
  - Shariah supervisory oversight


---

## 8. Structure Consistency

Every section should:
1. Define the problem
2. Explain structural solution
3. Explain regulatory alignment
4. Explain cross-border implication

Avoid repeating definitions already defined in DOMAIN_KNOWLEDGE.md.


---

## 9. Overclaim Control

Before finalizing any section, check:
- Are we claiming legal certainty?
- Are we claiming regulatory approval?
- Are we implying guaranteed returns?
- Are we overstating liquidity?

If yes → soften the language.


---

## 10. Style Guide

- Use structured headings.
- Use tables for comparison.
- Use bullet points for compliance logic.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Avoid academic literature review tone.
- This is a whitepaper, not a journal article.
