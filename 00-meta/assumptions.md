# Assumptions (Working Draft)

This document lists working assumptions used to draft the whitepaper. It is not legal advice and should be validated during legal structuring and regulator engagement.

## Market and Program Assumptions

- Minimum scope is ASEAN, with phased expansion by jurisdiction (Indonesia, Singapore, Malaysia at minimum).
- Pilot phase begins in Indonesia (domestic distribution and settlement) before cross-border rollout.
- Underlying real estate assets remain in Indonesia; no land title transfer via blockchain.
- Token represents economic rights only, defined by issuance documents and enforced off-chain.
- Distribution and secondary trading occur only through regulated pathways (where permitted).

## Compliance and Operating Assumptions

- KYC/AML and investor verification are performed off-chain by regulated/approved parties.
- Personal data is not stored on-chain; on-chain records use cryptographic references for integrity.
- Jurisdiction-aware whitelisting is mandatory for cross-border participation.
- The architecture supports regulator visibility via reporting and audit interfaces, subject to confidentiality and data protection requirements.
- Pilot may begin with a single Indonesian issuance vehicle (single SPV) to validate controls; scaling is achieved by reusing the same orchestration layer across multiple issuance vehicles (multi-SPV / series) without weakening controls.

## Liquidity Assumptions

- Tokenization does not guarantee liquidity; liquidity must be engineered through venue strategy, market structure, and documented redemption terms (if any).
- Liquidity fragmentation across ASEAN jurisdictions is expected and managed via venue-specific controls.

## Funding Model Assumptions

- The platform supports multiple configurations: debt, profit participation, sukuk, and crowdfunding.
- Each configuration requires separate legal documentation, risk disclosures, and regulatory alignment.
