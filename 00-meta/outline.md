# Whitepaper Outline (ASEAN Real Estate Tokenization)

This outline maps directly to the `01-draft/` files and is written for an ASEAN minimum scope with Indonesian underlying assets.

## 01. Executive Summary
- Strategic positioning and scope (ASEAN; Indonesia issuance hub)
- Economic-rights-only token model
- Hybrid compliance architecture (on-chain/off-chain)
- Funding models and liquidity engineering overview
- Implementation approach

## 02. Problem Statement
- Fragmented ASEAN capital access and distribution constraints
- Operational friction in issuance and lifecycle administration
- Liquidity and price-discovery limitations for private real estate exposure
- Data protection and cross-border compliance complexity

## 03. Scope / Non-Scope
- In-scope: regulated issuance and distribution for ASEAN participants
- Out-of-scope: land title transfer, permissionless DeFi, unregulated markets

## 04. Stakeholders
- Regulators, issuers, SPVs/DIRE/KIK structures, investors
- Distributors, exchanges, market makers, custodians, valuers, auditors
- Technology operators and compliance service providers

## 05. Architecture
- Hybrid compliance architecture and core components
- Token lifecycle events and control points (RBAC, sequential approvals)
- Regulator visibility and auditability

## 06. Cross-Border Design (ASEAN)
- Jurisdiction-aware whitelisting and selling restrictions
- Distributor/exchange integration patterns
- Reporting, disclosures, and cross-border operational governance

## 07. Privacy and Personal Data (UU PDP and ASEAN PDPA)
- Data minimization and off-chain personal data
- On-chain cryptographic references and audit trails
- Data controller / processor responsibilities and incident response

## 08. Liquidity Engineering
- Tokenization ≠ automatic liquidity
- Venue strategy, market structure, market making, NAV reference, redemption
- Monitoring metrics and fragmentation management

## 09. Funding Models
- Debt, profit participation, sukuk, crowdfunding configurations
- Comparative regulatory implications and investor protections

## 10. Shariah Considerations
- Sukuk structures (ijarah and variants), governance, oversight
- Disclosure and alignment with Shariah principles

## 11. Risks and Mitigations
- Legal/regulatory, operational, technology, market, privacy risks
- Controls, audits, and governance mitigations

## 12. Roadmap and Sandbox Strategy
- Phased rollout: Indonesia foundation → ASEAN expansion
- Sandbox approach, readiness gates, and implementation milestones

## 13. Appendix
- Control matrix (illustrative)
- Token lifecycle event catalogue (illustrative)
- Glossary pointers and reference placeholders
